Overcoming Failure

3 May 2019

On the 21 Feburary 2019, I received my GCE A levels result. It sucked. I obtained a total score of 65rp, an IGP of DDC/B. What followed by was bawling my eyes out for following few days...

I was despodent and dumbfounded.

What should I do? Should I settle on a private university? Should I retake as a private candidate? What if I don't improve even if i retook? What would others think of me?

I've never thought that I would do this badly. I was not expecting a 65rp at all as I studied really hard for my As (or as if i thought so)

After much self conflict, plastering myself on google search and hearing advices from several people, I came into a decision of retaking As. It wasn't an easy decision, but I realized I have to, rather than entering into an university course that i didn't have interest in, and then regretting it later in the future.

Wow... man having to to plan for my revision was hell. I didn't even know where to start studying, but I am very blessed to have a supportive family and my partner who constantly motivate and encourage me.

Fast forward to today, the 3rd month into re studying, I have completed the syllabus for Chemistry, 3/4 done with Math and Biology and have completed 3 TYS papers up to date.

I want to prove to myself and people around me that if you put your heart and soul into anything you want to accomplish, nothing is impossible.

This blog will encompass my journey of overcoming failure
